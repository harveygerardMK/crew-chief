# What You're Signing Up For — A Pacer's Guide

Thanks for considering pacing Harvey for a chunk of the Tahoe 200. Before you commit, here's the honest version.

## The basics

- **Pacing means running alongside Harvey** for one segment between aid stations, for safety and company. You're not allowed to carry his gear or give him food/water on trail.
- **Earliest you can start: mile 87.6 (Loon Lake).** All six potential pacer legs are between there and the finish.
- **Leg lengths range from 15 to 27 miles** and include 2,000–3,700 ft of climbing each.
- **You'll likely be running at night.** Headlamp is mandatory.

## What it actually feels like

By the time Harvey picks up his first pacer at Loon Lake, he'll have been running for roughly 30+ hours and 87.6 miles. He will be tired, possibly hallucinating, and not at his sharpest. Your job is:

1. **Keep him moving.** Especially uphill, especially when he wants to sit down.
2. **Keep him safe.** Navigation, footing, weather awareness.
3. **Keep him fed and hydrated.** You can't *give* him food or water on trail, but you can remind him to eat and drink at the next aid station and during stops.
4. **Be company.** Sometimes that's conversation. Sometimes it's silence. You'll figure it out.

## What you need to bring

The mandatory pacer gear is identical to the mandatory runner gear:

- Hooded jacket
- Gloves
- Hat or buff
- Pants or tights (full leg coverage)
- Long-sleeve insulating layer
- Headlamp or waist light + extra batteries
- Cell phone
- Whistle
- Emergency blanket
- 500 emergency calories
- Pacer bib (provided at check-in)
- Your own GPX of the course on your watch or phone

Strongly recommended on top of that:
- Trekking poles
- Puffy jacket
- 3 L water capacity
- Caffeine for yourself

## Logistics

- **You'll get to the aid station yourself.** Harvey's crew can help coordinate, but you're responsible for getting to your start point and getting home from your end point.
- **Sleep stations.** Beds at sleep stations are for runners first. If beds are available, pacers can use them, but you may need to give one up.
- **If you can't keep up.** Tell Harvey early. He'll stay with you to the next aid station. You can drop there.

## Bail-out points

If you start a leg and realize partway through that you can't continue, your only option is to push to the next aid station (Harvey stays with you). You can't quit on trail. Choose a leg you're confident in — Harvey would rather you take a shorter leg you'll finish than a long one you won't.
