# The Course

The 2026 Tahoe 200 is the full-loop course — a single 200.4-mile circle around Lake Tahoe via the Tahoe Rim Trail and connecting routes. Total elevation change is around 64,000 feet (gain plus loss combined).

## The loop, briefly

Starting from Heavenly Stagecoach Lodge on the south shore in Nevada, the course heads west and south through the Sierra at Tahoe area, then northwest into the Desolation Wilderness fringe (Wrights Lake, Loon Lake). From Loon Lake the route climbs east to Barker Pass on the west side of the lake, then traces north along the rim through Tahoe City and up to Brockway Summit. From Brockway it crosses to the east side of the lake at Village Green (Incline Village area), then drops south along the Nevada side to Spooner Summit before climbing back to the finish at Heavenly.

## Aid stations and pacing

Thirteen aid stations break the course into segments ranging from 7.5 to 19.4 miles. Three of them are sleep stations: Wrights Lake (mile 70.7), Barker Pass (103.1), and Brockway Summit (148.6).

Crew can access seven stations: the start/finish (Heavenly), Sierra at Tahoe, Loon Lake, Tahoe City, Brockway Summit, Village Green, and the finish. Pacers are allowed beginning at Loon Lake.

## What to expect

- Most of the course is on the Tahoe Rim Trail, which is well-marked baseline. The race adds reflective ribbons and arrow signs at intersections.
- Backcountry sections are remote — limited or no cell coverage.
- Water sources between aid stations can be 20 miles apart and may not always have water. Carrying 3 liters of capacity is recommended.
- Critical habitat for the endangered Sierra Nevada Yellow-legged Frog crosses the route. Stay on trails. Travel single-file.
