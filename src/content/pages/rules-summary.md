## Rules that actually matter

Full rules are in the runner manual. These are the ones that get me disqualified if crew or pacers slip — please read them.

## Crew

- **One vehicle per runner.** Max 20 ft × 8.5 ft. No bigger RVs/trailers. Crew pass in the windshield.
- **Crew access only at crew aids.** Don't meet me on trail. Don't meet me at non-crew stations. Either = DQ.
- **Park legally.** Not on vegetation, not blocking traffic or emergency access. Walk in if you have to park farther out.
- **No camping at aids.** When I leave, you leave.
- **Use Live Tracking** so you arrive within ~30 minutes of me. Parking is tight.
- **Bring your own food and water.** Aid food is for runners, active pacers, and volunteers.
- **Pack your trash out.** Aids aren't set up for crew garbage.
- **Be decent to volunteers.** They're volunteers. They can DQ me for crew behavior.

## Pacers

- **Pacing starts at Loon Lake (mile 87.6) only.**
- **One pacer at a time.** No tag-teaming on trail.
- **Wear the pacer bib** from check-in or any aid.
- **Carry the full mandatory runner kit for yourself.** Gear checks happen.
- **No muling.** Don't carry my gear, food, or water. Sharing food/water on trail (except real emergency) DQs me.
- **Your own GPX.** Don't rely on my nav.
- **If you can't keep up, I stay with you to the next aid.** You can drop there; I don't leave you on trail.

## Both

- **Sanitation.** Bathroom, outhouse, or Wag Bag — not the woods. Pack used Wag Bags to the next aid.
- **Sportsmanship.** Volunteers, locals, other runners — bad behavior from crew or pacers can DQ me.

## Medical

If medical pulls me, I'm out — and I can't volunteer, crew, or pace at this event again without medical-director clearance. If med staff says stop, please back them. The body is one thing; getting to start future races is another. Don't argue with the medics.
