# Rules That Matter — Crew & Pacers

The full rules are in the runner manual. These are the ones that will get Harvey disqualified if you mess them up, so please read them.

## For crew

- **One vehicle per runner.** Maximum 20 ft long × 8.5 ft wide. No RVs or trailers larger. Display the crew pass in the windshield.
- **Crew access only at crew-access aid stations.** Do not meet Harvey on the trail. Do not meet him at non-crew stations. Doing either = automatic DQ.
- **Park legally.** No parking on vegetation. No blocking traffic or emergency access. If you can't park legally near the station, park farther away and walk.
- **No camping at aid stations.** Once Harvey leaves, you leave.
- **Use Live Tracking** to time your arrival within 30 minutes of his arrival. Parking is limited.
- **Bring your own food and water.** Aid-station food is for runners, active pacers, and volunteers only.
- **Pack your trash out.** Leave No Trace. Aid stations don't have capacity for crew garbage.
- **Be nice to volunteers.** They're doing this for free and they can disqualify Harvey if you're rude.

## For pacers

- **Pacing starts at Loon Lake (mile 87.6) only.** Not before.
- **One pacer at a time.** No tag-teaming on trail.
- **Wear the pacer bib.** Pick it up at check-in or at any aid station.
- **Carry the full mandatory runner gear list, for yourself.** Pacer gear checks happen.
- **You cannot mule.** Don't carry Harvey's gear, food, or water. Sharing food or water on trail (except in an emergency) DQs Harvey.
- **Have your own GPX.** Don't rely on Harvey's navigation.
- **If you can't keep up, Harvey stays with you to the next aid station.** You can drop there, but he doesn't leave you on trail.

## For both

- **Sanitation.** Use bathrooms, outhouses, or Wag Bags. Do not just go in the woods. Pack used Wag Bags out to the next aid station.
- **Sportsmanship.** Treat volunteers, locals, and other trail users with respect. Bad behavior by crew or pacers can DQ Harvey.

## The medical rule

If Harvey is pulled by medical staff, he's out — and he can't volunteer, crew, or pace for anyone else at the event without medical-director clearance. If you find yourself in a situation where medical staff is recommending he stop, please support that recommendation. The body is one thing; getting future-race-eligible is another. Don't argue with the medics.
