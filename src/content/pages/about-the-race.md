# About the Tahoe 200

200.4 miles, one loop around Lake Tahoe, mostly on the Tahoe Rim Trail. Aspen meadows, alpine lakes, exposed ridges — the usual.

The clock runs up to 105 hours (4 days, 9 hours). It starts Friday morning and doesn't stop until you cross the line or time out.

## Why it's hard

- **No stopping the clock.** Three sleep stations (6 hours max each), but the timer keeps going.
- **Vertical.** 32,000+ feet up and 32,000+ feet down. Technical trail, remote sections.
- **Altitude.** Most of the course is 6,500–9,000 feet.
- **Weather.** I've seen race reports from 10°F to 90°F on the same course — rain, hail, snow, all plausible.
- **Sleep deprivation.** Hallucinations aren't a bug; they're part of the plan. The race director tells you to expect them.

## What finishing means

They call it an "Endurance Run," not a race. Under 105 hours you get a buckle and a finisher print. There are awards for top three, but most people are there for the finish, not the place.

## Why I'm doing this one

This is the big one in my 2026 season — after Ultra Trail Snowdonia (50K) and with UTMB on the horizon. Tahoe is four-ish days of mountain running at altitude on a course where help can be a long way away.

If you're following along, live tracking usually shows up closer to race day on the [official race site](https://www.destinationtrailrun.com/tahoe).
