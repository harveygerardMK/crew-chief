# About the Tahoe 200

The Tahoe 200 is a 200.4-mile single-loop mountain trail run that circumnavigates Lake Tahoe via the Tahoe Rim Trail, with detours through aspen meadows, alpine lakes, and high ridgelines.

It's run continuously over up to 105 hours — that's 4 days, 9 hours. The clock starts Friday morning and doesn't stop until runners cross the finish line or hit the cutoff.

## What makes it hard

- **It's nonstop.** Runners sleep at three designated sleep stations along the way (capped at 6 hours each), but the clock is always running.
- **The terrain.** 32,000+ feet of climbing and 32,000+ feet of descent. Exposed ridges, technical singletrack, remote backcountry.
- **The altitude.** Most of the course sits between 6,500 and 9,000 feet.
- **The weather.** Anything from 10°F to 90°F is possible. Past years have seen rain, hail, and snow on the same course.
- **Sleep deprivation.** Hallucinations are common and expected. Runners are told to plan for them.

## What "finishing" means

Officials call it an "Endurance Run," not a race. Finishing under 105 hours earns a custom belt buckle and a finisher's print. Top three men and women get additional awards, but the philosophy is that the accomplishment is the experience — not the placing.
