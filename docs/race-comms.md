## Group Text Protocol

Set up a group text with all pacers + crew before Thursday check-in. Include:
- Harvey's phone (he carries it but may not check often once on course)
- All crew members
- All pacers

Use the group text for:
- Harvey's position updates (screenshot from wheresharvey.com)
- "Harvey just left Sierra at Tahoe — estimated Loon Lake Sat 3–6 PM"
- Pacer coordination ("Gangle, Harvey is 2 hrs out from Barker Pass")
- Schedule changes

## Arrival Estimation Workflow

When Harvey leaves a crew stop, note the time. Then:

1. Check wheresharvey.com for last beacon position
2. Compare to known pace windows in `aid-station-crew-lists.md`
3. Text the next pacer their estimated pickup window
4. Crew departs for next stop with buffer time

**Rule of thumb:** If Harvey is running faster than baseline (closer to 72hr pace), shorten the crew sleep windows accordingly. Check wheresharvey.com when you wake up — if he's further than expected, drive immediately.

## Harvey Can't Reach Crew

If Harvey needs crew and there's no cell service:
1. He asks an aid station volunteer to relay a message
2. Aid stations have radio communication with race HQ
3. Race HQ can relay to crew via phone

**Harvey's instructions on course:** If separated or in trouble, stay on trail. Use GPX device. Signal with whistle. Conserve phone battery — do not call unless emergency.

Amanda posts updates to wheresharvey.com via the crew update form. This keeps family informed without requiring direct calls. Post updates at each major crew stop (Sierra, Loon Lake, Tahoe City, Brockway, Village Green, Finish).

## Radio / Satellite Option

If cell coverage is a concern for Loon Lake or Brockway communication with pacers, consider:
- **Garmin inReach Mini** — 2-way satellite messaging, no cell required. Harvey could carry one.
- Rental units available from outdoor gear shops in South Lake Tahoe.
