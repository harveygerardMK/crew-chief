## Harvey Wants to Drop

This is an emotional moment. The plan:

1. **Don't rush the decision** — a lot of DNF requests happen at low points that pass in 20–30 minutes after eating, sitting, and warming up
2. Get hot food and warm layers on him first. Don't discuss DNF while he's cold and hungry.
3. Ask: "What's actually happening with your body right now?" vs. "Are you okay?" — get specifics
4. If it's mental fatigue: remind him of the sleep strategy; the race has planned sleep stops
5. If it's physical: assess what specifically — feet, GI, cold, injury. Most have a fix.
6. If he says "I'm done" twice after eating and warming up, believe him and support it fully

**How to officially drop:**
- Notify an aid station volunteer — they radio race HQ
- Stay with Harvey until race staff confirms
- He keeps his SPOT tracker until race staff retrieves it
- Crew vehicle can drive him out from any crew-access station
- For non-crew stations: race staff arranges transport

**This does not make him less of a person. The course is 200 miles.**

## Medical Emergency

**First:** Contact race staff before calling 911, unless immediately life-threatening.  
- Race medical leads: Annette Florczak, Tasha Swartley, Julie Burges
- Aid station volunteers have radio contact with race HQ
- Rescue waits on this course can be 4–24 hours — race staff knows the terrain

**Key race rules:**
- IV during event = automatic DQ
- EMS/SAR use = automatic DQ (even if Harvey recovers and continues — the race is over)
- These rules exist for good reason; accept them

### Signs to take seriously
- Core temperature issues (shivering uncontrollably, confusion, slurred speech)
- Severe hallucinations (beyond mild visual disturbances — those are common after 50+ hours)
- Can't hold down any food or water for 2+ hours
- Significant injury (ankle, knee) that affects gait
- Medical director note: severe delusion may mean a pull; race staff makes the final call

### What crew does
1. Stay calm
2. Get Harvey warm, sheltered, and seated
3. Notify nearest aid station volunteer immediately
4. Call race medical leads
5. Stay with Harvey until race staff arrives

## Weather

### Afternoon thunderstorms (common in Sierra Nevada in June)
- Standard operating procedure: get off exposed ridges and peaks by early afternoon
- Harvey's mandatory waterproof jacket is for this
- Aid stations will have weather info
- If a storm cell is visible: find shelter and wait; do not press on above treeline

### Cold nights
- Night temps at elevation can drop to 30–40°F in June
- Harvey has warm layers in each drop bag and with crew
- Puffy jacket is mandatory kit
- If Harvey arrives at a crew stop severely cold: get him into dry warm layers before doing anything else

### Wildfire / smoke
- If AQI is high: prioritize the reusable dust mask (bag 4)
- Dust masks help with fire smoke too
- Race may issue guidance — follow race staff instructions

### Heat (afternoon miles 130–163)
- Tahoe City to Brockway is likely the hottest section (afternoon Sunday)
- Salty snacks, electrolytes, water volume all matter here
- Watch for heat illness signs: confusion, stopping sweating, nausea

## Crew Vehicle Issues

### Flat tire or breakdown
- Have roadside assistance info accessible (AAA, insurance, etc.)
- If breakdown happens between stops with no cell service:
  - Drive to nearest town if safe to do so slowly
  - Use emergency blanket if waiting overnight
- Notify pacers immediately if crew will be late to their pickup point
- Race org can sometimes relay messages via radio network at aid stations

### Wrong road / can't find station
- **Loon Lake**: Use Icehouse Rd. Wrights Lake Rd is a common wrong turn — it has no crew access.
- **Brockway**: Walk 0.6 mi from designated lot. Don't try to drive closer.
- **Tahoe City**: Transit Center parking. The aid station is 0.2 mi walk.
- Download offline maps before leaving cell range (Google Maps, CalTopo offline)

### Crew gets separated (two crew members, two cars — not applicable with single vehicle rule)
- One vehicle per runner. If there's only one vehicle, this is moot.
- If crew is two people in one car: set a clear rule that one person never leaves the other without the car

## Wildlife

- **Bears**: Active in Homewood area. Store food in hard-sided containers in the car overnight. Don't leave crew food out at stops.
- **Sierra Nevada Yellow-legged Frog habitat**: Stay on trail. Harvey has course marked on GPS.
- Standard bear encounter rule: make noise, don't run, back away slowly.
